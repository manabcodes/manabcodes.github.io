About Me

I am an interdisciplinary researcher from South Asia with interests ranging from technology to philosophy to geopolitics!

A graduate in computer science from the University of Dhaka in Bangladesh, I worked at Fujitsu Research Institute in Tokyo before joining BRAC University in my home country as a lecturer of mathematics and data science. I am a recent alumnus of the European Master's in Law, Data and AI from Dublin City University in Ireland and Universidad de León in Spain, and have also worked with INCIBE, the National Cybersecurity Institute of Spain, and the School of Religion, Theology, and Peace Studies at Trinity College Dublin as a researcher and technical consultant.

I am currently appointed as a doctoral candidate at the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid within the HARNESS Network under Dr. Elena Montiel-Ponsoda and Dr. Víctor Rodríguez-Doncel, focusing on the concept of sectoral and regional data spaces and their possibilities and limitations. When not investing time in studying technology policy, I read non-fiction on history, listen to podcasts, and travel extensively. I also speak six languages, but not all of them with equal fluency.
