About Me

I'm an interdisciplinary researcher from South Asia whose curiosity spans technology, philosophy, and geopolitics. I mean, why pick just one existential crisis to study?

My academic journey began with computer science at the University of Dhaka in Bangladesh, followed by work at Fujitsu Research Institute in Japan (where I learned that yes, the stereotypes about Japanese work culture are mostly true, and Tokyo is the greatest city on earth). I then returned home to teach mathematics and data science at BRAC University before pursuing a European Master's in Law, Data and AI across Dublin City University and Universidad de León — I have commitment issues when it comes to settling somewhere.

My research experience includes collaborations with INCIBE (Spain's National Cybersecurity Institute) and Trinity College Dublin's School of Religion, Theology, and Peace Studies, because nothing says "career focus" like jumping between cybersecurity and theology. Currently, I'm pursuing my doctorate at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid as part of the HARNESS Network, where I work under Dr. Elena Montiel-Ponsoda and Dr. Víctor Rodríguez-Doncel exploring sectoral and regional data spaces, their potential and constraints (spoiler alert: there are many constraints).

Beyond technology policy research, I'm passionate about history (preferably the dramatic bits), podcasts (I have strong opinions about which ones are overrated), and travel (which has resulted in some questionable visa photo updates over the years). Also a big fan of left-wing populist politics, trains, and breadtube. I can talk in six languages with varying degrees of fluency, from "fluent enough to argue about who will be the next prime minister in your country" to "fluent enough to order food without ending up asking if I can have the pillow sauce and the shampoo croquettes." I believe in the power of interdisciplinary thinking to tackle complex global challenges, because single-discipline thinking is way too straightforward for the mess we have created for ourselves.
